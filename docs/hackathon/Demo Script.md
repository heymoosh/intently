# Demo Script — Opus 4.7 Hackathon

**Type:** Creative/strategy — pitch narrative, demo arc, script drafts.
**Created:** 2026-04-21
**For:** 3-minute submission video due Apr 26, 8 PM EDT

---

## Core Pitch

*You're a PM who spent years delivering complex cross-functional work in an F500 with nothing but MS Office and a Gantt chart, where nobody remembered what they did last week — and you have ADHD, which means your brain also doesn't remember what you did last week unless you're interested. You built a personal operating system that works for both problems at once. Now you're generalizing it, with the insight that LLM agents have the exact same memory problem humans do: they lose the thread, forget the people, re-ask questions you already answered. A life ops layer that solves memory for humans and agents, by someone who had to solve it twice.*

---

## The Problem Statement We're Answering

**"Build From What You Know."**

The thing someone you know still does by hand: cobble together a personal ops system in spreadsheets, sticky notes, journals, and Google Docs because no tool actually fits how their brain works. Every ADHD PM, every working parent, every independent operator running multiple threads.

The thing LLM agents still can't do: remember you between sessions. Lose the thread mid-project. Re-ask questions you already answered.

Same problem. Different substrate. One system solves both.

## 3-Minute Demo Arc (First Draft)

### Open — 20 seconds
Muxin on camera, direct to viewer.

> "I have ADHD. I'm a PM. I've been building a system that works for my brain for years, because nothing off the shelf did. Along the way I figured out something: the memory problem LLM agents have — forgetting context, losing the thread, re-asking things — is the same problem humans like me have. So I built one system for both."

Cut to phone.

### Morning scene — 45 seconds
Lock screen → app open. Voice memo button.

> "Got a client call with Mitsui at 10. Need to prep the pitch. And I want to journal on yesterday's call with Zane — something's sitting with me."

Agent surfaces morning briefing. Not a checklist. A narrative brief:
- References yesterday's Zane call from memory
- Surfaces Mitsui prep with context from prior interactions ("last time they pushed back on pricing structure, not format")
- Notes a pattern: "you had three calls this week where the other side asked about timeline — worth pre-addressing today"

That last line is the Opus 4.7 moment. Shows memory + pattern recognition, not retrieval.

### Midday capture — 30 seconds
Voice memo after the client call, walking.

> "Mitsui loved the format. They want pricing by Friday. Zane's thing was about boundaries — I think I need to push back next time."

Agent splits the signal:
- Updates Mitsui project state, adds "pricing deliverable" task with Friday due
- Adds Zane preference update: "values direct boundary-setting; I should push back more"
- Journal entry captured with tone

### Evening debrief — 45 seconds
End of day. Agent surfaces reflection.

> Agent voice (text or TTS): "Today went long on client work and short on your own projects. The Mitsui momentum is real. The Zane conversation is the third time in two weeks you've noticed the boundary thing — worth a real journal entry, not just a tag. Tomorrow is lighter; I'd protect the morning for Lost Woods."

Muxin, on camera: "That's it. It remembers. It notices patterns. It knows what matters to me and it threads those things through the day."

### Close — 40 seconds

> "The part that surprised me most while building this? Agents don't actually struggle to remember facts. They struggle to remember *people* — the shape of how someone communicates, what they care about, what your relationship is. That's the thing we had to solve, for me and for Claude at the same time. That's what this is."

Cut to architecture shot or app screen with tagline.

**Tagline candidate:** "A memory system for humans and the agents that help them."

---

## Key Moments That Must Land

1. **Opening credibility:** the PM + ADHD frame has to feel real, not pitched. Understated beats polished.
2. **The "pattern across calls" moment:** this is the Opus 4.7 showcase. Can't just be retrieval — has to be synthesis.
3. **The evening reflection:** narrative, not checklist. Shows the memory stack working.
4. **The closing insight:** "agents don't struggle with facts, they struggle with people." Needs to feel earned, not telegraphed.

---

## Production Notes

- **Shoot on phone.** Authentic > polished. The product is a mobile app; filming on the same device signals alignment.
- **Minimal edits.** Cuts between scenes, lower thirds maybe. No stock music. Quiet ambient sound or silence.
- **Voice:** Muxin's real voice throughout. No AI voiceover. The pitch is about a human who built something — the human should be heard.
- **Screen recording:** record actual app interactions, not mockups.
- **B-roll allowed:** walking with phone, closing laptop, evening ambient — short inserts only.

---

## Script Notes / Beats to Experiment With

- Open with the ADHD framing or open with the agent parallel? Both work. ADHD is more human; agent parallel hooks the Anthropic audience. Try both.
- Show one client/one person deeply, not many shallowly. Mitsui + Zane is enough.
- Resist the temptation to showcase every feature. 3 minutes = one arc, one insight, one emotional beat.
- The closing line is the anchor. Write it first; write backward from there.
